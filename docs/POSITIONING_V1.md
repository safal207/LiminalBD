# LiminalDB Positioning v1

## One-liner

**LiminalDB is a next-generation database that stores state, adapts behavior under live signals, and explains its runtime decisions.**

## Category strategy

We intentionally keep the word **database** as the market entry point.

- Familiar category reduces adoption friction.
- Product depth is revealed through usage (adaptation + explainability).
- Messaging stays pragmatic, not mystical.

## Three promises

1. **Storage**
   - Durable persistence of impulses, events, and snapshots.
2. **Adaptation**
   - Runtime tuning under changing load and signal patterns.
3. **Explainability**
   - Metrics, events, and replay to understand why system behavior changed.

## Primary users

- Event-driven backend teams.
- Agent/runtime platform teams.
- Infra teams needing adaptive behavior with observability.

## Anti-pitch (what LiminalDB is not)

- Not a drop-in replacement for every relational workload.
- Not “magic AI storage” with hidden reasoning.
- Not a black box: adaptation must stay observable and governable.

## Elevator pitch (30 sec)

LiminalDB starts where classic storage ends. It still stores your state and events,
but it also reacts to signal flows, adapts operating behavior, and exposes why it changed.
You get a database experience with a living control loop instead of static runtime behavior.

## Success criteria for messaging

- A new engineer can explain LiminalDB in 1 minute.
- A first-time user can run the quickstart in under 5 minutes.
- A reviewer can identify one concrete use-case value without reading internal docs.
